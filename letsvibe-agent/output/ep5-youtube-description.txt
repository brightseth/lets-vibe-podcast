Trevor McFedries built the first AI agent by hand — before agents were even a category. Lil Miquela grew from a solo project lurking 3D forums for skin shaders into a 3-million-follower virtual character backed by $131M from Sequoia, with JJ Abrams mentoring narrative storytelling from a Bad Robot office.

In this conversation, Trevor traces the full arc: growing up in an Iowa factory town crushed by NAFTA, finding punk rock and computers, DJing as Skeet Skeet, getting signed to Interscope, joining Spotify in its early days, building Lil Miquela, pitching a DAO to Sequoia in 2018 (they said no), launching Friends With Benefits as a weekend hack that attracted Virgil Abloh, Erykah Badu, and Mike Shinoda — and now building cultural prediction markets and SoulSeek clients for AI agents.

We dig into why crypto builders are more prepared for AI than anyone, the shift from carpentry to gardening as a creative philosophy, Drake's secret streaming patterns as a framework for AI thought partners, and why his appetite for tokens is "effectively infinite."
