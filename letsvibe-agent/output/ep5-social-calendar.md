## Day 2

### LinkedIn

Before the term "AI agent" entered the mainstream, Trevor McFedries spent years building one by hand.

Lil Miquela started as a solo project — lurking 3D forums for skin shaders, learning narrative storytelling from JJ Abrams at Bad Robot, growing an Instagram character from 500 followers to 3 million. That journey led to $131M from Sequoia and a team of nine.

In Episode 5 of Let's Vibe!, Trevor traces the line from Iowa factory town to LA creator economy to crypto DAOs to the current AI moment. His perspective is unusually grounded: he's shipped across music (Spotify early days, DJ Skeet Skeet, producing for Banks), virtual characters, and decentralized communities (FWB attracted Virgil Abloh, Erykah Badu, Mike Shinoda).

Three insights that stayed with me:

1. Crypto builders are more prepared for AI than anyone. Composability, sybil resistance, vaporware detection — these are the same problems the AI ecosystem is now confronting.

2. The shift from carpentry to gardening. You can will things into existence, or you can work with the universe and accelerate what's already emerging. Trevor argues the best builders learn to do both.

3. Revealed preference is the unlock for AI thought partners. People will explore ideas with an AI that they'd never pitch in a room — and that vulnerability is where the most original creative work lives.

Trevor is now building Runner (cultural prediction markets on blockchains) and SoFTT Seek (a SoulSeek client where agents download MP3s). His take: the pipes being built for high-throughput blockchains won't carry derivatives — they'll carry novel cultural markets we haven't imagined yet.
